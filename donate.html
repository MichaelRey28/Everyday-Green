Our Campaigns

Tree Planting

Both people and the environment depend on trees. Numerous studies have shown that urban nature and trees can enhance people's mental and physical health, children's attention and test scores, a neighborhood's property values, and more. Urban areas are kept cool by trees.

Donate Trees

There are so many advantages that trees bring to our daily lives. They help prevent climate change, filter clean air, produce clean water, and provide habitats for countless plant and animal species. The Earth can be protected from climate change and biodiversity loss by planting a billion trees. We remove carbon from the atmosphere and promote biodiversity when we restore and conserve key forests. Although a billion is a large number, we are confident that we can accomplish it as a team.

Recyling

In order to safeguard our planet, the department of the environment has been focusing its campaign on environmental preservation and awareness utilizing various strategies like planting trees, trash segregation, reuse, reduce, and recycling. Reuse, Reduce, and Recycle are then put to use in this situation. Our priorities as responsible citizens should include protecting the environment. We can live in harmony with the 3Rs of the environment in a variety of ways.

Saving Water

To maintain the water ecosystem and to meet present and future human demand, water conservation includes the policies, strategies, and practices used to manage fresh water as a sustainable resource. The amount of water utilized depends on many factors, including population, household size and growth, and wealth. Pressure on natural water resources, particularly in manufacturing and agricultural irrigation, will increase as a result of factors like climate change.

Save Forest

The stability of the environment, as well as human health and happiness, depend on forests, which are like the lungs of the planet. Half of all terrestrial species on Earth are found in these spectacular and ancient environments. And yet, forests are under attack, particularly in tropical areas. An alarming amount of logging, mining, unsustainable agriculture, and development are destroying our planet's forests. The crisis's sheer scope is mind-boggling; if current deforestation rates continue, tropical rainforests may vanish within the generation of today's children. Fortunately, there are options available to us.
